Four Digs

EXCAVATION 01
QUALITATIVE RESEARCH · STAKEHOLDER COORDINATION

Voice of Customer Program

The Brief

Hospitality field sellers were the closest source of buyer truth. There was no formal feedback loop and no mechanism for routing what they were hearing back into messaging or strategy.

The Method

Designed and facilitated quarterly VOC calls across three hospitality tiers (Select, Premium, Luxury). Structured each conversation around competitive pressure, buyer motivations, content gaps, and emerging trends. Synthesized findings into segment-specific insight summaries shared back across product, sales, and marketing.

The Payoff

Insights routed into messaging, sales enablement assets, and direct campaign concepts, including the inspiration for the segment's first full-funnel integrated campaign.

EXCAVATION 02
DATA AUDIT · ANALYTICS COLLABORATION

Data Quality Diagnostic

The Brief

Segmentation across the hospitality contact database was guesswork. Something was wrong, but nobody had quantified what.

The Method

Audited the contact database. Identified roughly 50% of records missing job titles. Partnered with RevOps and ZoomInfo to design and run a targeted enrichment program against priority accounts and segments.

The Payoff

Role-based segmentation became possible for the first time. Outreach got more relevant. The cleaner data also became the foundation for a behavioral funnel-stage tracking system in Pardot, replacing one-size-fits-all messaging.

EXCAVATION 03
SECONDARY RESEARCH · NARRATIVE BUILDING

Strategic Research Synthesis

The Brief

$700K+/year key account RFP. The customer wanted a strategic partner, not a vendor. A standard product pitch would lose the room.

The Method

Synthesized third-party research into a consultative narrative: wellness real estate trends, demographic shifts, strength training growth, layout philosophy, and ADA considerations. Built the strategic frame that anchored every subsequent design, technology, and product session.

The Payoff

The narrative held through the full pitch cycle. Set the tone for a partnership-led conversation rather than a feature-and-spec one.

EXCAVATION 04
PROJECT FACILITATION · CONSENSUS BUILDING

Senior Stakeholder Workshop

The Brief

Senior leadership team from a major global hospitality brand was visiting. A standard demo would not differentiate. The opportunity was to move from vendor to strategic partner.

The Method

Designed and helped run structured workshops: brand matrix mapping across the customer's full portfolio, persona development by sub-brand, and "wow moments" ideation. Captured insights live and translated them into a positioning framework organized by brand identity.

The Payoff

The customer called the company "the hospitality company" and asked for strategic partnership, not vendor work. Workshop output became a positioning framework plus a slate of 2026 campaign concepts.
